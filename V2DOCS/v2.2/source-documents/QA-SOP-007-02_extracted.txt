   OBJECTIVE:

        The purpose of this Standard Operating Procedure is to  establish  a
        comprehensive, well-controlled,  risk-based,  and  lifecycle-managed
        system  governing  the  creation,  formatting,   review,   approval,
        issuance, reconciliation, revision, archival, retrieval, protection,
        and controlled destruction of all GMP-relevant documents and records
        at Atlantic Lifesciences Ltd, such that all  documented  information
        remains accurate, contemporaneous, traceable, tamper-protected,  and
        inspection-ready throughout its entire retention lifecycle.


   SCOPE:

                This SOP applies to all  documented  information  generated,
        received, processed, reviewed, approved, or retained within ALS that
        relates to GMP systems and QMS of the company. These include  paper-
        based documentation, hybrid documentation, electronic documentation,
        and audit trails.


   DEFINITIONS AND Abbreviations:


4 Definitions:


5 Controlled Document


        A formally approved instruction, specification, method, or procedure
        that is issued under QA authority and subject to version control and
        controlled distribution.


7 Record

        Documented evidence demonstrating that an  activity,  test,  review,
        decision, or verification step has been performed in accordance with
        approved procedures. Once data is entered, the document  transitions
        from “document” to “record.”

8 Master Copy

        The officially approved and  archived  original  version  maintained
        exclusively under QA documentation control.

9 Controlled Copy


        A traceable issued copy distributed for  operational  use,  recorded
        within document issuance logs.


11 Uncontrolled Copy

        An uncontrolled copy is a document  provided  for  information  only
        that is not tracked, updated, or retrieved by the  document  control
        system and therefore shall not be used to perform GMP activities.

12 Display Copy


        A Display Copy is an authorized photocopy of the Master  copy  of  a
        document that is placed at a specific location within  the  facility
        for the purpose of guiding personnel in  the  correct  execution  of
        operations or procedures.


14 Obsolete Document

        A superseded version withdrawn from operational use but retained for
        historical traceability until end of its defined retention period.

15 Product Lifecycle

        The stages of  a  product’s  life  from  pharmaceutical  development
        through technology transfer, commercial manufacturing,  and  product
        discontinuation.

     2. The structure shall be from top (highest  authority/broadest  scope)
        to bottom (lowest, most specific).
     3.  Level-I  documents  define  the  overall  QMS,  quality   policies,
        objectives, and organizational structure.
     4. Level-II documents shall include detailed,  step-by-step  procedures
        for activities affecting product quality. They define how to perform
        tasks.
     5.  Level-III  documents  are  specific,  technical   instructions   or
        requirements for a particular process or material.
     6. Level-IV documents include completed documents that provide evidence
        that activities were performed according to procedures.


29   Preparation and Numbering of Document

     7. All Level I-IV documents shall be prepared as per  their  respective
        SOPs.
     8. Preparation of new documents shall be initiated through the  process
        of change control as per SOP No: QA/SOP/006.
     9. All documents  shall  be  numbered  as  per  the  SOP  for  Document
        Numbering (SOP No. QA/SOP/002.
    10. QA shall verify the assigned document number  for  correctness  upon
        receipt of the Change Control form with the attached  document  from
        the initiating department for review. Documents  shall  be  reviewed
        thoroughly as per the procedure outlined in section 5.3.
    11. Documents shall be prepared in soft copy to be printed on either  A4
        or A3 size paper. Layouts/Drawings may be printed on A0,  A1  or  A2
        size paper as per requirement.
    12. The formatting requirements (including font type, font  style,  font
        size, font colour, page border, line spacing, and paragraph spacing)
        shall  be  the  same  as  defined  in  the  SOP  for  SOP  (SOP  No.
        QA/SOP/001).
    13. All documents shall be prepared mainly in English Language.
    14. Page numbering format shall be Page X of Y. For example,  a  Logbook
        having 20 pages will be paginated as Page 1 of 20, Page 2 of 20…  up
        to Page 20 of 20.
    15. Documents should have unambiguous contents: the title,  nature/type,
        and purpose (where applicable) should be clearly stated.
    16. After preparation of any document, such document shall be  subjected
        to review prior to approval.

30 Document Review and Approval Workflow

    17. No GMP document shall be issued unless it  has  undergone  technical
        review by the Head of User Department or subject  matter  expert(s),
        quality system compliance review by QA, and final approval by  Head-
        QA/Designee.
    18. All new GMP documents and any revisions to  existing  GMP  documents
        that  may  affect  product  quality,  patient   safety,   regulatory
        compliance,    validated    processes,    data     integrity,     or
        interdepartmental workflow shall undergo a  cross-functional  review
        prior to final approval and issuance.
    19. QA shall initially  perform  an  impact  assessment  on  the  Change
        Control  Form  to  identify  all   departments   whose   operations,
        responsibilities,  equipment,  systems,   records,   or   regulatory
        commitments may be affected by the document.
    20.  Cross-functional  documents  shall  be  reviewed  by  the  HODs  or
        qualified representatives from all concerned departments.
    21. The Document owner (User department) shall submit the draft document
        in soft copy (Ms  Word  version)  to  all  impacted  departments  as
        indicated in the Change Control Form  for  cross-functional  review.
        The draft document shall  be  clearly  marked  “DRAFT”  as  diagonal
        watermark on each page.
    22. The reviewing department shall display from  the  beginning  of  the
        document, the “Comments” pane and  keep  “Tracked  Changes”  on.  To
        enable these features in Ms  Word,  the  following  steps  shall  be
        followed:
   a) To display Comments pane, click on Review > New Comment
   b) To keep Tracked changes on: click on Review > Track Changes  >  Track
      Changes
    23. Each reviewing department shall conduct a structured  evaluation  of
        the draft document to confirm that:
     a) the procedure is technically accurate and  consistent  with  current
        validated processes,  approved  specifications,  current  regulatory
        guidelines, and equipment capability (where applicable);
     b) the procedure is operationally feasible and can be executed  without
        ambiguity or procedural conflict;
     c)  responsibilities,  authorization  levels,   and   interdepartmental
        interfaces are clearly defined and acceptable;
     d) the document  does  not  introduce  unacceptable  risks  to  product
        quality, sterility assurance, patient safety, cold chain  integrity,
        or data integrity;
     e)  referenced  forms,  records,  systems,  protocols,  guidelines  and
        related SOPs exist and are correctly identified.
     f) Spelling and Grammar check is complete, and there  are  no  spelling
        errors or grammatically incorrect statements.




    24. Document Reviewer Feedback
     1.       All  reviewers  shall  provide   comments,   suggestions,   or
        corrections using the Comments pane of the document and return it to
        the Document owner after review.
     2.     The document owner, in coordination with QA,  shall  consolidate
        all reviewer comments  and  ensure  that  required  corrections  are
        incorporated. Where conflicting technical opinions arise,  QA  shall
        coordinate resolution discussions, and  the  final  agreed  decision
        shall be applied to the document.
     3.     The reviewed draft document shall be modified to incorporate all
        agreed  changes,  and  such  modifications  shall  be  traceable  to
        reviewer comments. Where  substantial  technical  modifications  are
        made that could affect previously  reviewed  sections,  the  revised
        draft  shall  be   re-circulated   to   affected   departments   for
        confirmation of acceptance prior to approval.
     4.     Cross-functional review shall be considered complete  only  when
        designated departmental representatives  have  formally  signed  and
        dated  the  document,  thereby  confirming  that  the  procedure  is
        technically acceptable, operationally feasible, and  compliant  with
        applicable GMP requirements.

    25. Quality Assurance Final Review
      1. Following completion of cross-functional review, QA shall perform a
         final independent verification to confirm that:
           a) all required reviews are completed,
           b) document numbering and version control are correct,
           c) referenced documents are valid and current,
           d) training requirements are identified where applicable.

    26. Authorization/Approval for Issue of Documents
      1. Final authorization for document release shall only be  granted  by
         the QA Head or authorized  designee  after  confirmation  that  the
         cross-functional review process has been fully completed.
    27. The reviewed document shall be printed on A4-size paper  or  A3-size
        paper where appropriate.
    28. All authorized signatories, including the author,  the  reviewer(s),
        and the approver, shall append their signatures and date in blue ink
        at the approval page/sections of the document.
    29. The approved document shall  then  be  issued  by  QA  to  the  User
        Department for training purpose. The responsible  QA  Officer  shall
        write the “Issue Date” at the designated section.



        31 Training on Approved Document

    30. Wherever required,  training  shall  be  imparted  as  per  SOP  No.
        QA/SOP/003, to concerned personnel within fifteen (15) working  days
        after issuance of the approved document.
    31. After training has been completed, the User Department shall  return
        the issued document to QA to be made effective.
    32. The QA Officer  responsible  for  document  control  shall  apply  a
        ‘MASTER COPY’ stamp in blue ink at the top left corner of the Header
        on each page, sign, and write the Effective Date in the Master  Copy
        stamp.

32 All master documents shall be stored with the QA department  in  a  sheet
        protector and well-archived to protect them from damage, loss,  pest
        infestation and deterioration due to  environmental  conditions  and
        unauthorized access.



33 Control of Documents


34 The User Department shall raise a request for issuance  of  documents  by
        completing the Document Request Form (Format No:  QA/SOP/007/F03-02)
        and submitting it to QA.


35 The QA  Officer  in-charge  shall  review  the  request  information  and
        approve if found satisfactory, by signing with date.


36 The required number of copies shall then be photocopied from  the  Master
        copy and stamped as “CONTROLLED COPY” and “ISSUED BY QA” in blue ink
at  the  top  right  corner  and  bottom-right  corner,  respectively.   The
        responsible QA person shall sign and indicate the Issue date in  the
        “ISSUED BY QA” stamp.


37 Each copy of the individual document shall be issued with a  unique  copy
        number, as mentioned below

               YY/DC/NNNN
      Where YY: Indicates the last two digits of the calendar year
      DC: Indicates the Department Code
      NNNN: Represents the serial no. of  a  specific-format  copy  starting
        from ‘0001’.

38 The copy number shall be appended on the top right corner  on  the  first
        page of the format.


39 In the case of formats with multiple pages, which  shall  be  stapled  or
        bound together, the “ISSUED BY QA” stamp shall  be  applied  on  the
        first page only with sign & date, while all other  subsequent  pages
        shall be controlled by applying the “CONTROLLED COPY”  stamp,  which
        shall  also  be  on  the  first  page.  This  is  to  prevent   page
        substitution and ensure data integrity.


40 However, documents with loose sheets to be issued  as  unbound  documents
        shall have the “ISSUED BY QA” stamp applied  on  each  page  at  the
        bottom right corner.


41 QA shall issue all Level IV documents, and the issuance details shall  be
recorded  in  “Daily  Document  Issuance,  Retrieval  and  Disposal  Record”
        (Format No.: QA/SOP/007/F01-02).


42 For Level II & III documents, such  as  Specifications,  MOA,  Protocols,
        Reports, and Laboratory note books, entry shall be made in the “Non-
        Routine Document Retrieval and Disposal Record” logbook (Format  No.
        QA/SOP/007/F02-02) and the Controlled copies shall be distributed to
the User HOD/Designee, who shall append his/her  signature  as  evidence  of
        receipt.


43 In  case  of  computer-generated  formats  (such  as  SOP  Master  Index,
       Equipment Master List), the soft copy of the  format  shall  be  used
       directly as  a  template  by  providing  the  required  data  without
       altering the structure or sections  of  the  approved  template.  The
       completed document shall then be  printed  and  controlled  with  the
       MASTER COPY stamp,  and  copies  made  for  distribution  shall  have
       CONTROLLED COPY stamp applied by QA as appropriate.


44 Soft copies of all quality documents shall be stored  and  maintained  by
       the QA documentation team for backup and future retrieval.


45 Reproduced documents should be clear and legible and without errors  from
the reproduction process.


46 When auditors who are conducting on-site audits  request  for  documents,
       any document issued (usually controlled copies)  shall  be  retrieved
       after  completion  of  the  audit  or  review  activity  by  the   QA
       responsible person and returned to  the  original  storage  location.
       The same shall apply to documents  requested  by  other  persons  for
       reading, evaluation or due diligence purposes. All  recipients  shall
       be required to maintain confidentiality and shall  not  reproduce  or
       distribute the documents without prior authorization from QA.






        48 Issuance of Uncontrolled Copies


49 Documents which may be requested by external parties such as  regulators,
third-party auditors,   vendors  or  service  providers  shall  be  sent  as
       Uncontrolled copies, except for dossier submission.


50 Uncontrolled copies issued for reference or  information  purposes  shall
       be clearly identified as such and shall not be used  for  operational
       purposes.


51 Such uncontrolled copies shall be photocopies of the original  or  master
       documents, which shall be stamped at the top  right  corner  of  each
       page as ‘UNCONTROLLED COPY’ in blue ink by QA.






        53 Issuance of Display Copies


54 Display copy of documents shall be made by photocopying the  Master  copy
       and applying the “DISPLAY COPY” stamp at the top right corner.


55 Whenever display copies of a document is required,  the  User  Department
       shall make a request using the Document Request Form, and this  shall
       be reviewed, approved, and issued by QA.


56 Upon receipt, the User Department shall place the Display copies  at  the
       designated operational areas  where  the  document  is  required  for
       reference during routine activities.


57 Issuance of display copies  shall  be  recorded  in  the  Daily  Document
       Issuance, Retrieval, and Disposal Record to  ensure  proper  tracking
       and control.


58 When a document is revised,  superseded,  or  withdrawn,  all  previously
       issued display copies shall be promptly retrieved and  replaced  with
       the updated version.



59 Issuance of Logbooks


60 The user  shall  request  Logbook(s)  using  the  Document  Request  Form
       (Format No. QA/SOP/007/F03-02).  Upon  receipt  of  the  request,  QA
       responsible person shall review, approve, and record the  details  in
       the Logbook Issuance Record (Format No. QA/SOP/007/F04-00).


61 Each Logbook shall be assigned a unique number to  identify  and  control
       same. Once a number  is  allotted,  the  same  number  shall  not  be
       reassigned to any other logbook. The  number  shall  be  as  per  the
       format:

       LOG/XX/DC/YYYY
       ➢ Where XX indicates the last two digits of the calendar year
       ➢ DC: Indicates the Department Code
       ➢ YYYY: represents serial no. of logbook starting from ‘0001’.

62 The Logbooks shall be prepared from the respective formats and  paginated
in the format Page X of Y, where Y represents the  total  number  of  pages,
       including the cover page. For example, for  a  50-page  logbook,  the
       page numbering shall begin from Page 1 of 50, Page 2 of 50…up to Page
       50 of 50. Each page shall be controlled by  applying  the  CONTROLLED
       COPY stamp  on  the  top  right  corner.  This  is  to  prevent  page
       substitution and ensure data integrity.


63 The Cover Page of the Logbook shall be as per the  template  provided  in
       Format No. QA/SOP/001/F05-00 if portrait presentation is desired,  or
       Format No. QA/SOP/001/F06-00, if a landscape presentation is desired.



64 Logbooks shall either be stapled or comb-bound, depending on the size.


65 After recording relevant details, QA Officer in charge  shall  issue  the
       logbook to the user who shall sign  to  acknowledge  receipt  of  the
       document.


66 Laboratory notebooks shall be treated as  controlled  GMP  documents  and
       shall be issued by QA to the respective Quality Control Analysts upon
receipt of duly completed Document Request Form.


67 Each Laboratory notebook shall have a unique  identification  number  and
       issuance shall be recorded in the “Non-Routine Document Retrieval and
Disposal Record” logbook.


68 Completed laboratory notebooks shall be reviewed by  the  Lab-QA  Officer
       before archival and shall be in accordance with the approved document
retention schedule.



69 Control of Records (Reports Generated from Approved Templates)


70 All reports or records generated  using  approved  templates  or  formats
       shall  be  considered  GMP  records  upon  completion  and  shall  be
       maintained as  documented  evidence  of  activities  performed.  Such
       records shall  comply  with  ALCOA+  principles,  and  shall  clearly
       indicate the report title, report number (where applicable), date  of
       preparation,  and  signatures  of  the  responsible   personnel   for
       preparation, review, and approval.


71 Records generated in soft copy and subsequently printed shall be  derived
only from current  approved  templates/formats  without  alteration  of  the
       template structure or content. Upon printing, such records shall  not
       be  stamped  as  “MASTER  COPY”  or  “CONTROLLED  COPY”,   as   these
       designations apply only to controlled documents. Reports shall not be
       subject  to  version  update  and  shall   not   require   controlled
       distribution tracking. The authenticity and  control  of  the  record
       shall be ensured through proper completion,  signatures,  pagination,
       and traceability to the originating template version.


72 Copies of completed records generated for internal  circulation,  review,
       or reference shall be clear, complete, and legible  reproductions  of
       the original record. Such copies shall not be stamped as  “CONTROLLED
       COPY”. The original record shall be  securely  maintained  by  QA  in
       accordance with the approved retention schedule to ensure  protection
       against loss, damage, or unauthorized access.


73 Revision of Documents


74 Documents shall be revised in accordance with the  stated  review  period
       of each document as per their respective SOPs.   The  generic  review
       period shall be every three years.


75 Any revision with a change shall undergo change control procedure as  per
SOP No. QA/006. No document should  be  changed  without  authorization  and
       approval.


76 Summary of changes in the document shall  be  provided  in  the  Revision
       History section of the document.


77 Retrieval and Archiving of Documents


78 The user department shall submit executed/completed documents to  the  QA
       department for archiving.


79 Un-executed/Superseded logbooks shall  also  be  submitted  to  QA  after
       verification.


80 Whenever a format is revised or obsoleted, all the  previous  un-executed
       formats shall be reconciled by the User department.


81  Upon  SOP/format  revision,  all  existing   related   logbooks/formats,
       controlled copies, and  display  copies  shall  be  retrieved  before
       issuance of the revised format/logbook. A new logbook shall be issued
only upon submission of the old logbook.


82 In some exceptional cases, a maximum of two logbooks  can  be  held  with
       the user department, and a new logbook shall  be  issued  only  after
       submission of the old executed logbook. Example: In the 1st  logbook,
       some of the continuous  entries  are  still  pending,  then  the  2nd
       logbook can be issued. Before  the  3rd  logbook  issuance,  the  1st
       logbook should be submitted to QA.


83 Before making SOP obsolete, all related formats  and  logbooks  shall  be
       retrieved from users.


       Note: If any recording or data entries are initiated in the  existing
       format, the same can be utilized until closure of respective entries.
       However, further columns/pages/rows of the logbooks/formats shall  be
       marked as ‘NA’.


85 All Logbooks issued to functional departments shall be replaced with  new
logbooks at the end of every year +1 month.


86 The user department shall  complete  the  empty/left  over  spaces/fields
       available in the  old  log  books  by  following  good  documentation
       practices and shall submit the books to QA for archiving.

               Note:  To  facilitate  issuance  of  new  logbooks  to   user
       departments for the upcoming year, QA Documentation shall  issue  the
       required number of "Log book issuance records" to record the issuance
       details  of  upcoming  year  log  books   required   for   functional
       departments (with upcoming numbering pattern) in the  existing  year,
       and the same log books shall be used until the completion  of  books.




       87 Retention of Documents


88 All GMP documents and records shall be retained  for  specific  retention
       periods  to  ensure  traceability,  regulatory  compliance,  and  the
       ability to reconstruct all manufacturing, testing, distribution,  and
       quality-related activities associated with products  manufactured  or
       marketed by the company.


89 Batch manufacturing, packaging, testing, and distribution  records  shall
       be retained for one year after expiry of  the  batch  or  five  years
       after batch certification, whichever is longer.


90 Records supporting  marketing  authorization,  including  validation  and
       stability data, shall be retained for the  entire  lifecycle  of  the
       product’s marketing authorization.


91 Electronic records shall remain accessible and  readable  throughout  the
       retention period  and  shall  be  preserved  using  validated  backup
       systems.


92 Specific retention periods for all GMP documents  and  records  generated
       in ALS have been provided in Annexure No. QA/SOP/001/A01-00.


93 Obsolete Master copies of documents shall be retained until  the  end  of
       their retention period.



94 Destruction of Documents


95 Approved GMP documents shall only  be  destroyed  after  their  retention
       period has expired and upon written authorization from QA.


96  Being  the  custodian  of  most  GMP  documents,  QA  may  initiate  the
       destruction of master documents that have  outlived  their  retention
       period or documents that are draft copies or obsolete  controlled  or
       uncontrolled copies. However, a formal request for destruction of any
document  shall  be  made  by  the  User  Department  using  the   “Document
       Destruction  Request  Form”  (Format   No.   QA/SOP/001/F07-00)   and
       submitted to QA  for  review  and  approval  before  the  destruction
       process is initiated.


97 Prior to destruction, QA  shall  verify  that  the  applicable  retention
       period  has  been  met  in  accordance  with  the  approved  Document
       Retention Schedule.


98 All documents approved for destruction shall be recorded in  a  “Document
       Destruction Register” (Format No. QA/SOP/001/F08-00), indicating  the
       document  title,  document  number,  revision   number,   originating
       department, date of destruction, and responsible personnel.


99 Documents shall be destroyed using  secure  methods  such  as  shredding,
       pulping, incineration, or validated electronic  deletion  to  prevent
       reconstruction or unauthorized access.


100  Destruction  activities   shall   be   witnessed   by   an   authorized
       representative of  QA  Department  to  ensure  compliance  with  this
       procedure.


101 Third-Party Destruction Services: When external  service  providers  are
       used for document destruction, they  shall  be  approved  by  QA  and
       appropriate  records  or  certificates  of   destruction   shall   be
       maintained.


102  Legal  and  Regulatory  Hold:   Documents   associated   with   ongoing
       investigations, complaints, recalls, legal proceedings, or regulatory
inquiries shall not be destroyed (even if  they  are  past  their  retention
       period) until written clearance is provided by QA where applicable.


103 Document destruction shall be suspended  during  regulatory  inspections
       or internal investigations if the records may be required for review.


104 Records of document destruction shall be retained as controlled  quality
records and maintained for the life of the company.
