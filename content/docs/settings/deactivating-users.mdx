When someone leaves your organisation or no longer needs access, you deactivate their account instead of deleting it. This keeps all of their historical data intact.

---

## What Happens When You Deactivate Someone

- They are immediately logged out of the app
- They cannot sign in again
- They see a message saying their account has been deactivated if they try
- All of their existing records stay in the system — acknowledgements, signatures, maintenance logs, and audit trail entries are all preserved

<Callout type="info">
  Accounts are never permanently deleted. This is by design — regulations often require that records of who signed what and who performed which tasks are kept indefinitely, even after someone leaves.
</Callout>

---

## How to Deactivate a User

1. Go to **Settings** → **Users**
2. Find the user in the table
3. Open their action menu (three dots)
4. Click **Deactivate**
5. Confirm the action

---

## How to Reactivate a User

If someone returns or was deactivated by mistake:

1. Go to **Settings** → **Users**
2. Find the deactivated user (they will appear greyed out)
3. Open their action menu (three dots)
4. Click **Reactivate**
5. They can now log in again with their existing credentials

---

## When to Deactivate

- When an employee leaves the company
- When a contractor's engagement ends
- When someone moves to a role that no longer requires platform access
- As a temporary measure during investigations or extended leave
