Your digital signature is used when signing Change Controls.

---

## Viewing Your Signature

Go to **Settings** → **My Profile**. Your signature is shown in the profile summary.

---

## Re-drawing Your Signature

You can manage your signature at any time by navigating to **Settings** → **My Profile** and clicking **Re-draw Signature**. 

You have three options for creating your digital signature:

### Option 1: Draw on Screen
Using your mouse or trackpad, you can manually draw your signature directly in your web browser. Follow the on-screen guides to ensure your signature is centered.

### Option 2: Upload an Image
If you already have a digitized version of your signature, you can upload it.
- **Allowed Formats:** PNG (Transparent recommended) or JPG
- **Max File Size:** 2MB

<Callout type="tip">
  For the best appearance on Change Control certificates, use a transparent PNG image with a dark pen color.
</Callout>

### Option 3: Sign with Mobile (Recommended)
You can use your smartphone to capture a high-quality touchscreen signature and securely beam it directly to your desktop.

1. Select the **Mobile** tab in the signature dialog.
2. A secure QR code will be generated on your screen.
3. Open your smartphone's camera and scan the QR code.
4. Your phone will open a secure signing page. Use your finger or a stylus to draw your signature.
5. Tap **Submit Signature**.
6. The signature will automatically sync with your desktop browser—no apps or accounts required on your phone!

<Callout type="info">
  Mobile signing sessions expire automatically after 15 minutes for security purposes. If your session expires, simply close the dialog and generate a new QR code.
</Callout>

---

## Security and Privacy

<Callout type="danger">
  Your signature is stored securely using hardened Row-Level Security (RLS) policies. It is only accessible by the platform and is never shared externally.
</Callout>

Updating your signature affects **future** Change Controls only. Past signature certificates are immutable and will always display the signature image you used at the exact moment of signing.
