SOP-Guard Pro is a platform for managing **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)** and **Preventive Maintenance (PM)** tasks. It is not a document editor — instead, it helps your organisation make sure the right procedures reach the right people at the right time. Every document change is tracked, and digital signatures are required before updates go live.

---

## How the App is Organised

SOP-Guard Pro has two main areas:

1. **The SOP Library** — Where all your procedures live. Every SOP is here, organised by department. You read, search, and acknowledge SOPs from this central place.

2. **The Equipment Registry** — Where all your maintained assets are stored. Each piece of equipment has its own record, linked to the maintenance tasks that keep it running.

Everything else in the platform supports these two areas:
- **Approvals** and **Change Control** manage the review and sign-off process for SOPs
- **The Pulse** sends you notifications about SOPs and equipment that need your attention
- **Reports** collect data from both areas for compliance tracking
- **Messaging** lets you discuss any record with your team

> **Note:** There are no separate department pages in the app. Your department is part of your profile — it controls which SOPs and equipment you see, but there is no "department homepage".

---

## Who Uses SOP-Guard Pro?

There are four types of users:

- **Employees** — Read and acknowledge SOPs, complete maintenance tasks, and receive notices. This is the most common user type.

- **Managers** — Everything an Employee can do, plus submit SOPs for approval, sign Change Controls when SOPs are updated, add equipment, and reassign maintenance tasks.

- **QA Managers** — The quality gatekeepers. They approve SOP submissions and equipment additions before anything goes live. A QA Manager is simply a user in the QA department with the Manager role.

- **Administrators** — Manage the organisation's setup — adding departments, inviting users, and configuring settings. Admin is an extra permission that can be added to either Employee or Manager accounts.

---

## The Pulse — Your Work Feed

The Pulse is the panel on the right side of the screen that keeps you informed about everything that needs your attention. It shows:

- **Priority items** — Approvals waiting for you, Change Control signatures due, overdue maintenance tasks
- **Today's items** — Tasks due today, SOPs that have been updated
- **Notices** — Announcements from managers and admins
- **Personal to-dos** — Items you have added for yourself

The Pulse updates automatically. When something new arrives, it appears here — you do not need to check different pages to stay on top of your work.

---

## How Quality is Guaranteed

Every Active SOP in the system has been reviewed and approved by a QA Manager. This cannot be changed — it is built into how the platform works:

- When a Manager submits a new SOP, it goes into the QA review queue
- A QA Manager reviews it and either requests changes or approves it
- Only after QA approval does an SOP become available to everyone

The same process applies to equipment — new assets must be approved by QA before they enter the maintenance schedule.

---

## What SOP-Guard Pro Does NOT Do

- **Document editing** — SOPs are written in Microsoft Word and uploaded as `.docx` files. SOP-Guard Pro shows them in a read-only viewer.

- **Email replacement** — For team conversation, use the built-in Messaging system. For time-sensitive announcements, use Notices via the Pulse.

- **HR management** — User accounts are managed here, but the platform does not track HR data like contracts, leave, or performance.

- **General file storage** — Only SOP documents, equipment photos, and maintenance completion photos are supported.
