SOP-Guard Pro is an industrial compliance platform designed for managing **Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs)** and **Preventive Maintenance (PM)** tasks. It is not a document editor — instead, it serves as a governance and distribution system that ensures the right procedures reach the right people at the right time, with full audit trails and digital signatures for changes.

## Two Central Hubs

SOP-Guard Pro is built on a **star-centralized architecture**. Two modules form the centre of everything:

1. **The SOP Library** — The central repository for all procedures. Every SOP lives here, organized by department. Users read, search, and acknowledge SOPs from this central hub.

2. **The Equipment Registry** — The register of all maintained assets. Each piece of equipment has its own record, linked to the PM tasks that keep it running.

Every other module in the platform orbits one of these two hubs:
- Approvals and Change Control manage the lifecycle of SOPs
- The Pulse delivers notifications about both SOPs and equipment
- Reports draw data from both hubs
- Messaging lets team members discuss any record in either hub

> **Note:** There are no department pages or department-specific sections. Department is an attribute of a person and a tag on a record — not a destination in the app.

## Who Uses SOP-Guard Pro?

The platform has four user types:

- **Employees** read and acknowledge SOPs, complete PM tasks assigned to them, and receive notices. They represent the largest user group.

- **Managers** do everything an Employee can do, plus submit SOPs for approval, sign Change Controls when SOPs are updated, add equipment to the registry, and reassign PM tasks.

- **QA Managers** (Quality Assurance) are the gatekeepers of the system. They are the only users who can approve SOP submissions and equipment additions before they go live. A QA Manager is simply a user in the QA department with the Manager role.

- **Administrators** manage the organisation's structure — adding departments, inviting users, and configuring settings. Admin is a flag that can be added to either Employee or Manager accounts.

## The Pulse — Your Work Feed

The Pulse is the **primary interface** for knowing what needs your attention. It lives on the right side of every page and shows:

- **Priority items** — approvals waiting for you, Change Control signatures due, overdue PM tasks
- **Today's items** — PM tasks due today, SOPs that have been updated, approval responses
- **Notices** — broadcast announcements from managers and admins
- **Personal to-dos** — items you have added for yourself

The Pulse updates in real-time. When something needs your attention, it appears here — you do not need to check specific pages to stay on top of your work.

## The Compliance Guarantee

Every Active SOP in the system has been reviewed and approved by a QA Manager. This is not a setting that can be changed — it is **built into the platform's fundamental design**:

- When a Manager submits a new SOP, it enters the QA review queue
- A QA Manager reviews it and either requests changes or approves it
- Only after QA approval does an SOP become Active

The same process applies to equipment — new assets must be approved by QA before they enter the maintenance rotation.

## What SOP-Guard Pro Does NOT Do

- **Document editing** — SOPs are written in Microsoft Word and uploaded as `.docx` files. SOP-Guard Pro renders them read-only.

- **Email replacement** — For team conversation, use the built-in Messaging system. For time-sensitive announcements, use Notices via the Pulse.

- **HR management** — User accounts are managed here, but the platform does not track HR data like contracts, leave, or performance.

- **Arbitrary file storage** — Only SOP documents, equipment photos, and PM completion photos are supported.
