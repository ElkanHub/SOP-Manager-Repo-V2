Notices are **broadcast announcements** — they go one way from the sender to the audience. Unlike messages in the Messaging module, notices are for announcements that do not require back-and-forth discussion.

---

## What is a Notice?

A notice is different from a message:

- **Message** — A conversation between two or more people. Replies create a thread.
- **Notice** — A one-way broadcast. Recipients can reply once (inline), but there is no extended conversation.

Use notices for:
- Announcing policy changes
- Sharing meeting reminders
- Broadcasting safety alerts
- General team updates

For ongoing discussions, use the Messaging module instead.

---

## Sending a Notice

1. Scroll to the bottom of the Pulse
2. Click **Send Notice**
3. Fill in the form:
   - **Audience:** Choose who receives it
     - **Individual** — Search for a specific person
     - **My Department** — Everyone in your department
     - **Everyone** — All users in the organisation
   - **Subject:** Required, up to 100 characters
   - **Body:** Required, up to 500 characters
4. Click **Send**

The notice appears in the Pulse for all recipients.

---

## Replying to a Notice

When you receive a notice, you can reply inline:

1. Click the notice in the Pulse to expand it
2. Type your reply in the text box that appears
3. Press **Enter** to send

Your reply appears as a threaded response to the original notice.

<Callout type="info">
  Replies are limited to one level deep. You cannot reply to a reply. For extended discussion, start a conversation in Messaging.
</Callout>

---

## Notice Best Practices

- **Keep it brief** — Notices are announcements, not documents
- **Use the right audience** — Department for team news, Everyone for organisation-wide updates
- **Be specific** — Include relevant dates, times, and action items in the body
