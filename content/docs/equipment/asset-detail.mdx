When you click on any equipment row in the Equipment Registry, you open its detail page. This page shows you everything about that piece of equipment and its maintenance history.

---

## Equipment Information

At the top of the page, you will see the key details:

- **Asset ID** — The unique identifier (e.g., EQ-001)
- **Name** — What the equipment is called
- **Department** — Which team is responsible for it (shown as a coloured badge)
- **Status** — Whether it is Active, Pending QA, or Deactivated
- **Serial Number** — The manufacturer's serial number (if provided)
- **Model** — The equipment model (if provided)
- **Photo** — An image of the equipment (if uploaded)

---

## Next Service Card

This card shows you when the next maintenance task is due. It uses colour coding to help you plan:

- **Green** — Due date is more than 3 days away
- **Amber** — Due within the next 3 days
- **Red** — Overdue — maintenance is past due

---

## Logging Maintenance (PM) Completion

If maintenance has been done, you can log it directly from this page:

1. Click the **Log PM Completion** button
2. Add your notes describing what was done (required)
3. Optionally upload a photo of the completed work
4. Click **Complete**

After logging, the system automatically calculates the next due date based on the equipment's maintenance frequency and creates a new task for the next cycle.

---

## Service History

Below the next service card, you will see a table listing every maintenance event for this equipment:

- **Date** — When the task was due or completed
- **Status** — Completed, Pending, or Overdue
- **Completed By** — Who logged the completion
- **Notes** — What was done

This gives you a complete maintenance timeline for compliance and reference.

---

## Linked SOP

If there is a procedure linked to this equipment, you will see a link to it. Click to open the SOP directly in the Library viewer — useful when you need to reference the maintenance procedure while working.

---

## Who Can See This Page

- Everyone in the equipment's primary department
- Users who belong to any of the equipment's secondary departments
- QA Managers (who can see all equipment across all departments)
