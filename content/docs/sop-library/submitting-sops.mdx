Managers submit SOPs for QA review. There are two types of submission:

- **New SOP** — A brand new procedure
- **Update** — Changes to an existing Active SOP

---

## Who Can Submit

Only **Managers** can submit SOPs. Employees can read and acknowledge, but the submission and approval process is reserved forManagers and QA.

---

## File Requirements

- **Format:** Microsoft Word (.docx) only
- **Maximum size:** 25MB
- **Content:** Write your procedure in Word, then upload

<Callout type="info">
  SOP-Guard Pro is not a document editor. You write procedures in Microsoft Word and upload them here. The platform handles governance, distribution, and acknowledgements.
</Callout>

---

## Submitting a New SOP

1. Go to **SOP Library**
2. Click **Upload SOP**
3. **Step 1:** Upload your .docx file
4. **Step 2:** 
   - Select "New SOP"
   - Enter SOP Number (e.g., SOP-015)
   - Enter Title
   - Select Primary Department (auto-filled to your dept)
   - Optionally add Secondary Departments
5. **Step 3:** Add Notes to QA (optional but recommended)
6. Click **Submit for QA Review**

Your SOP enters the queue as **Pending QA**.

---

## Submitting an Update

1. Go to **SOP Library**
2. Click **Upload SOP**
3. **Step 1:** Upload the updated .docx file
4. **Step 2:**
   - Select "Update Existing SOP"
   - Search for and select the SOP to update
5. **Step 3:** Add Notes to QA (describe what changed)
6. Click **Submit for QA Review**

<Callout type="warning">
  If the SOP has a Change Control in progress, it is locked. You cannot submit an update until the Change Control completes.
</Callout>

---

## After Submission

- You receive a **Pulse notification** when QA responds
- If QA requests changes, you can resubmit with the corrections
- If QA approves:
  - **New SOPs** go directly to Active
  - **Updates** trigger a Change Control

<Callout type="tip">
  Write specific, actionable notes in the Notes to QA field. "Updated safety thresholds in Section 3 following new regulatory guidance" is far more useful to a QA reviewer than "minor updates". Good notes lead to faster approvals.
</Callout>
