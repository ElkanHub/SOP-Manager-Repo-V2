The **SOP Library** is the heart of SOP-Guard Pro — it is where all Standard Operating Procedures live. Think of it as your organisation's procedure manual, always up-to-date and accessible to everyone who needs it.

---

## Default View

When you open the SOP Library, you see **Active SOPs from your department** by default. This focused view ensures you see only the procedures relevant to your work, without noise from other departments.

The table shows:
- **SOP Number** — Unique identifier (e.g., SOP-001)
- **Title** — Procedure name
- **Department** — Which team owns it
- **Version** — Current version (e.g., v1.0)
- **Status** — Current state
- **Date Listed** — When it went active
- **Due for Revision** — When it needs review

---

## Status Badges

Each SOP has a status that tells you its lifecycle stage:

<StatusBadge status="active" /> **Active** — The current operative version. Everyone in the department can read and acknowledge it.

<StatusBadge status="draft" /> **Draft** — Created but not yet submitted for QA review. Only visible to Managers in the owning department.

<StatusBadge status="pending_qa" /> **Pending QA** — Submitted for review, waiting for QA Manager approval. Only visible to Managers in the owning department and QA.

<StatusBadge status="pending_cc" /> **Pending CC** — QA approved, Change Control in progress. The SOP is locked and awaiting manager signatures.

<StatusBadge status="superseded" /> **Superseded** — Replaced by a newer version. Accessible via Version History.

---

## The Two-Layer Visibility Rule

SOP-Guard Pro has a deliberate two-layer approach to visibility:

1. **Default view** — Shows only SOPs from your department (plus any secondary departments you belong to)
2. **Search** — Searches across **all Active SOPs** in the organisation

This means:
- Your day-to-day view is focused on your department
- You can still find procedures from other teams when you need them
- Cross-department SOPs are read-only (you can read them but not acknowledge them)

---

## Tab Viewer

When you open an SOP, it opens in a tab within the Library. You can have multiple SOPs open at once, each in its own tab. This makes it easy to reference one procedure while reading another.
