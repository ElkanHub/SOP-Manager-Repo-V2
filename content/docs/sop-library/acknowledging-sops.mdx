When you acknowledge an SOP, you create a formal record confirming that you have read and understood the procedure. This is important for compliance — your organisation can prove who has read critical procedures.

---

## When to Acknowledge

The **Acknowledge** button appears in the SOP viewer header when:

1. The SOP is **Active**
2. The SOP belongs to **your department** (or a secondary department you belong to)
3. You have **not yet acknowledged** this version

If the SOP is from another department (found via cross-department search), you will see a "Read only" badge — cross-department SOPs cannot be acknowledged.

---

## How to Acknowledge

1. Open the SOP from the Library
2. Find the **Acknowledge** button in the header bar
3. Click it

A confirmation dialog appears. Click **Confirm** to complete your acknowledgement.

---

## What Happens After

Once acknowledged:
- The button is replaced by a green confirmation badge
- It shows "Acknowledged v[X]" with the version number
- The date of acknowledgement is recorded

Your acknowledgement is now part of the compliance record. Managers can run reports to see who has acknowledged which SOPs.

---

## Re-acknowledgement

When an SOP is updated to a new version, **your old acknowledgement does not carry over**. You will see the Acknowledge button again for the new version.

This is by design — each version may contain different procedures, and your acknowledgement applies to a specific version only.

---

## Where Acknowledgements Are Reported

Managers and QA can run the **Acknowledgement Log** report (Reports → Acknowledgement Log) to see:
- Which SOPs have been acknowledged
- Who acknowledged them
- When they acknowledged
- Which versions were acknowledged

This is useful for compliance audits and for tracking down who still needs to acknowledge an updated procedure.
