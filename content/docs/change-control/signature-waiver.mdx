Sometimes a required signatory is unavailable — on leave, departed, or otherwise unable to sign. In these cases, an Admin can **waive** the signature requirement.

---

## When to Waive

Waive a signature only when:

- The signatory is on extended leave
- The signatory has left the organisation
- The signatory is unavailable for other valid reasons

Use sparingly — signatures exist for a reason.

---

## Who Can Waive

Only **Admins** can waive signatures. This is intentional — it is a high-privilege action that requires admin access.

---

## How to Waive

1. Open the Change Control
2. Find the required signatory who is unavailable
3. Hover over their row
4. Click **Waive**
5. Enter a reason for the waiver
6. Confirm

---

## What the Waived Row Shows

After waiving:
- The row shows "Waived by [Admin name]"
- The reason is recorded
- The progress bar updates

---

## Audit Trail

Every waiver is permanently logged:
- Who waived (admin)
- Whose signature was waived
- The reason
- Timestamp

This cannot be removed. The audit trail exists for compliance.

<Callout type="danger">
  Every waiver is permanently logged with the admin's identity, the waived user, the reason, and the timestamp. This cannot be removed.
</Callout>
