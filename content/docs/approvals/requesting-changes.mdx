If a submission needs improvements, you can request changes. This sends feedback to the submitter with specific guidance on what to fix.

---

## When to Request Changes

Request changes when:

- The procedure is unclear or incomplete
- Steps are missing or incorrect
- Formatting does not meet standards
- Safety procedures are inadequate
- Any other issue that needs correction

Be specific in your feedback — vague comments lead to resubmissions that still need work.

---

## How to Request Changes

1. Open the submission to view the **Request Details Modal**.
2. Review the document and requester information in the main content area.
3. In the right-hand **Processing Panel**, click the **Request Changes** action button.
4. A text input area will expand seamlessly in the panel.
5. Write your feedback — be specific, actionable, and refer to exact sections if needed.
6. Click **Submit Feedback**.

The submitter instantly receives a Pulse notification and can see your feedback in the timeline of the request modal.

---

## Writing Useful Feedback

<Callout type="tip">
  Use the review thread as working notes. Document what you checked, what was acceptable, and what needs changing. This creates a clear record for both parties.
</Callout>

Good feedback:
- "Section 3.2 - The safety check step is missing. Please add verification that guards are in place."
- "The emergency shutdown procedure needs more detail. Add specific shutdown sequences."

Poor feedback:
- "This needs work"
- "Not good enough"

---

## What the Submitter Sees

The submitter receives a Pulse notification. When they open the submission, they see:

- Your change request in the thread
- The status changes to "Changes Requested"
- They can now resubmit with corrections

---

## Resubmission Process

When the submitter addresses your feedback:

1. They upload a corrected file
2. A new submission entry is created
3. You review the new version
4. The thread shows the full history

This cycle continues until you approve or reject.
