SOP-Guard Pro enforces a strict, multi-stage approval lifecycle for all documents to guarantee compliance and quality control. This is the end-to-end journey of an SOP submission.

---

## 1. Draft Phase

When a Manager uploads a new SOP, it is initially categorized as **Draft**. 
- It is only visible to the submitter and other Managers inside that specific department.
- The document can be freely edited, updated, or deleted without any audit trail consequences.

## 2. Pending QA

When the submitter clicks **Submit for Approval**, the SOP is locked and its status changes to **Pending QA**.
- The Quality Assurance team receives a notification.
- QA personnel use the Request Modal to review the document details.
- QA can either **Approve**, **Reject**, or **Request Changes**.

If changes are requested, the SOP bounces back to the submitter, and a threaded conversation begins within the modal. This loop continues until QA provides an approval.

## 3. Pending Change Control (CC)

<Callout type="info">
  SOPs that only have grammatical corrections may not require a full Change Control, depending on organizational procedures, though the platform defaults to requiring one for compliance.
</Callout>

Once QA approves the document, it enters the **Pending CC** state.
- The system generates an official Change Control certificate.
- The Change Control routes to all required Managers in the designated Department.
- The system captures immutable Digital Signatures from each requested Manager.

## 4. Active

Only after QA has approved the payload, AND all required Managers have signed the Change Control document, does the SOP become **Active**.
- The old version (if updating an existing SOP) is marked as **Superseded**.
- The new active SOP immediately appears in the general Library.
- Acknowledgement pulse notifications are blasted to all involved Employees.

This robust workflow ensures that unverified procedures are never enacted by ground-level employees without proper administrative oversight.
