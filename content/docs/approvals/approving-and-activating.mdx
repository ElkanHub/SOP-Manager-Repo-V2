When a submission meets your standards, you can approve it. What happens next depends on whether it is a new SOP or an update.

---

## When to Approve

Approve when:

- The procedure is clear and complete
- All steps are accurate
- Safety procedures are adequate
- It meets your organisation's standards

You are signing off that this procedure is ready for use.

---

## How to Approve

1. Open the submission
2. Review the document
3. Click **Approve**
4. Choose version bump:
   - **Minor version** (e.g., v1.0 → v1.1) — For small changes
   - **Major version** (e.g., v1.0 → v2.0) — For substantial changes
5. Confirm approval

---

## What Happens Next

### New SOP
- The SOP becomes **Active** immediately
- The submitter is notified in Pulse
- All department members can now read and acknowledge it

### Update (Change Control)
- A **Change Control** is created
- The SOP is **locked** (editing disabled)
- Managers in the department must **sign** the Change Control
- Only after all signatures does the new version go Active

<Callout type="info">
  Approving a SOP update is not the final step — it triggers the Change Control process. The new version only goes Active after all required managers have signed.
</Callout>

---

## Submitter Notification

In both cases, the submitter receives a Pulse notification:
- For new SOPs: "Your SOP [title] has been approved and is now Active"
- For updates: "Your SOP update has been approved. A Change Control has been issued for manager signatures."
