The **Calendar** gives you a visual overview of what is happening and when. It shows two types of entries:

1. **Maintenance Dates** — These appear automatically based on your equipment's maintenance schedules. You do not need to create them.
2. **Manual Events** — Events that you or your colleagues create for meetings, reminders, and team activities.

---

## How to Read the Calendar

Each day that has events shows small coloured chips. The colours tell you what type of event it is:

| Colour | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Blue** | A maintenance (PM) task is due on this date |
| **Green** | An event created by someone in your department |
| **Grey** | A public event from another department |

Click on any date to see a detailed list of everything scheduled for that day.

---

## Navigating Between Months

- Use the **left and right arrows** at the top of the calendar to move between months
- **Today's date** is always highlighted so you can quickly find where you are
- The current month loads by default when you open the Calendar

---

## Upcoming Events Panel

On the right side of the Calendar, you will see an **Upcoming** panel that lists everything due in the next 7 days. This is useful for quick planning without needing to click through individual dates.

---

## Who Sees What

- You see all maintenance dates for equipment in your department
- You see events created by your department members
- You see public events created by other departments
- QA Managers see maintenance dates across all departments
