Maintenance dates appear on the calendar automatically — you do not need to create them. The system calculates them based on each piece of equipment's maintenance frequency.

---

## How Maintenance Dates Appear

When a piece of equipment is approved and becomes Active, the system creates its first maintenance task based on the Last Serviced date and the frequency setting. This task appears on the calendar as a **blue chip** on the day it is due.

Each time a maintenance task is completed and logged, the system automatically calculates the next due date and creates a new calendar entry.

---

## Clicking a Maintenance Date

Click any blue chip on the calendar to see:
- **Which equipment** the task is for
- **Who is assigned** to complete it
- **The due date** and whether it is on time or overdue

From here, you can go directly to the equipment detail page to log completion or view the full maintenance history.

---

## Using the Calendar for Planning

The calendar helps you plan your week and manage workload:

- **See upcoming work** — Quickly find which days have the most maintenance tasks
- **Identify bottlenecks** — If too many tasks fall on the same day, talk to your Manager about reassigning some
- **Track patterns** — Notice which equipment needs the most frequent attention

<Callout type="tip">
  Check the calendar at the start of each week to see what maintenance work is coming up. This helps you plan your time and make sure nothing is missed.
</Callout>
